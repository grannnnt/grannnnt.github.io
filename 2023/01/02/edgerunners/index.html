Take You to the Moon

  • to Lucyna and David

It’s been a really long time since I was so moved. For me, who yearns for the universe and space but can only stay on this planet, the story of Lucy and David brought me an unprecedented touch. Two people, so fragile in their deep insides, rely on each other to move forward in the cold-blooded and cruel Night City. My dream is to make your dream come true. With such a love, the distant and even slightly absurd moon seems to be within reach. It is a romance beyond reality and my imagination.

In some sense, David’s own desire and that stubbornness are also the reasons why there was not a happy ending of the two. David’s vow of “Take you to the moon” is partly out of love, and partly out of the desire to realize self-worth. But such a setting also makes the story a lot more realistic. After all, sometimes it is really difficult for people to change, and love is also mixed with self. But on the other hand, it seems that this ending is also inevitable. This really reminds me of the story of Oedipus the King. David, might be really a tragic hero.

已经很久没有这么感动过了。对于无限憧憬宇宙和太空但又身处尘世的我，Lucy和David的故事带给我一种前所未有的触动。两个内心深处原本如此脆弱的人在冷血残酷的夜之城相互依靠着前行。我的愿望就是实现你的愿望。在如此的爱情下，那么遥远甚至略显荒诞的月球仿佛也触手可及。真的是一种超越现实和想象的浪漫。

从某种意义上来说，David自身背负的愿望和那种固执也是让两人的结局无法完满的原因。“带你上月球”的誓言，一部分出自爱情，一部分出自渴求自我价值的实现。但这样的人物设定也让故事多了很多现实感。毕竟有时候人真的很难改变，而爱情本也参杂着自我。但另一方面，似乎这种结局也是一种必然。不由得让人想起Oedipus the King的故事。David，真的是一位悲剧英雄吧。
